    Many apps aim to help their users improve their lives by giving them insight into their behavior. These apps track, quantify and visualize users’ activity at work, in the gym and at home to nudge them towards more healthy and productive lives. The goal of the project is to build up a collection of design patterns, and to learn how self-tracking apps collect and visualize data by designing innovative solutions in new innovative self-tracking application.
    The Philosophy and Professional Practice research group: This research group analyses new technologies and the design practice, technological culture and society from which these emerge. This reveals underlying values, interests, assumptions and world views. By reflecting on the use of technology in professional practice and daily life, the research group creates awareness of the impact of technology on people, professions and society as a whole, including the associated professional and ethical dilemmas.

    ABOUT THE PROJECT

    PROBLEM

    People are fundamentally unaware of the meaning of the data that current self-tracking apps are collecting. This is because the meanings defined by each app are different and ambiguous. Moreover, it is necessary to analyze how these data should be interpreted visually.

    Research questions for quantitative research below
    • What elements should self tracking apps have that can lead users to positive behavior?
    • How can users continue to use self tracking apps so that they can improve their lives?
    • How can data collection be visualized effectively in the dashboard?

    DEFINE PHASE

    INSIGHT

    1. Self entertainment, Self association, Self design, Self discipline and Self healing. Among these, the self tracking app must have an element that can motivate users to manage their lives independently rather than just following the self tracking app.
    2. Rather than encouraging mechanical definition of humans, self tracking app should strive for collective optimization unlike uniformization of people.
    3. One of the other negative aspects of using self tracking is the punitive aspect of not meeting the goal. It is not reasonable to face self condemnation if user tries but fail to achieve their goal.
    4. Unique strategy is needed that are different with other self-tracking apps.
    5. Image and table are easier to understand data than text on the dashboard. This can effectively compare how much productivity has improved compared to the past.

    PERSONA

    The design should have data visualization of collecting user’s data and awaken enough motivation that help to achieve the final goals in their lives. These involve self efficiency, endeavor, passion and accomplishment. With this immersing self tracking technology, people can create better experiences by stimulating self tracking.
