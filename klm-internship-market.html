    ABOUT THE PROJECT

    The project is the web app, which will provide helpful information and guidance for the students who will join the internship market. The event is called Internship Market and is held annually, usually 1-2 months before the start day of the internship intake. There are two intakes in the year, in February and September. In the internship market, students can meet all the teams/departments in KLM E&M that are opening for intern positions. Also, they can have a tour around the Schiphol Oost area and talk with the employees about the working life and working environment.

    PROBLEM

    At the MRO Lab of the KLM Engineering and Maintenance department, the onboarding process for the new interns is uninteresting and lacks information. They realise that the current onboarding is causing a drop in excitement and even stress for the new interns. After receiving the acceptance, no further information or factors can keep their engagement on the new journey at KLM. The excitement of the new interns is dropping with time, and on the first day of the new career, they do not feel interested anymore.

    In this onboarding process, I will focus on designing a digital platform that could inform the students about the internship market and different opportunities for the internship at KLM E&M and help them keep track of the information and the status of their application.

    • What are the needs and desires of the new interns when they are in the onboarding process?


    • What are the main problems that the current onboarding process is having at the moment?


    • Which media source could be used to communicate with the new interns during onboarding?

    RESEARCH QUESTIONS

    RESEARCH INSIGHTS

    • The onboarding is now dull and lacks information and instructions.
    • The onboarding time is missing frequent communication.
    • The need to promote internship market/internship opportunities to students.
    • New interns’ complaints revolve around giving/receiving information and long waiting times.
    • Limited digital interactions on the internship market event.
    • Students cannot keep track of the application process due to a lack of communication from the company.

    PERSONA

    The first persona is Kim Wierden; she is a 22-year-old communication student. Currently, she is looking for an internship opportunity at KLM since she is interested in the inclusive vision at KLM. However, there are not many advertisements online about KLM careers besides the KLM website. Therefore, her need is to have more options to view the internship changes online, which could be through social media.


    The second persona is about Rene Noot, a student who is now a new intern at KLM E&M - MRO Lab. He just got the offer from the managers and is now in the onboarding process. However, Rene feels that the long waiting time is dragging his enthusiast down because there is no more information after he gets the result. He wonders what he should do to prepare for the job and on the first day at work.

    To address these issues, the platform will:

    • Provide real-time status tracking for applications.
    • Use interactive digital media to keep interns engaged throughout the onboarding process.
    • Offer frequent updates and notifications about the internship process via multiple communication channels (e.g., email, app notifications).
    • Host virtual tours and Q&A sessions to make the onboarding experience exciting.
