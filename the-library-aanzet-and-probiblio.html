    The client is looking for new ways to change the look and feel of libraries, so children feel more attracted to visiting them. Furniture is also being changed so that it is more appealing and reflects and resonates the contents of books in surprising ways, inside and outside the library.
    Aanzet: The public Library Aanzet organizes the public libraries for 11 municipalities around Dordrecht. (13 libraries, 13 service points, 4 pick-up points). Bringing availability of collections of books and other media and cultural programming in libraries – and school buildings is one of their main goals. The public is diverse and aged 0-100. The library is innovative and playful.
    Probiblio: Probiblio is a provincial full-service organization for public libraries in the provinces of North- and South Holland. They support all library organizations in these two provinces. Probiblio supports education and are committed to innovation in local libraries.

    ABOOUT THE PROJECT

    PROBLEM

    The main problem is that children find it a dull and boring place and not a place for inspiration or a place that fosters their reading needs, as well as the motivation to come back to the library. We believe that not having a thematic or a different and more stimulating decoration and environment, it adds to the problem. Most children between the ages of 8 to 12 feel that they don't have a place to be that caters their needs due to it mostly caters for toddlers, these accompanied by the lack of gathering and socialising places as well as quiet reading spaces.

    1. There are common objects found in each library.
    2. Children's sections are dull and uninspiring with little room for interactions that spark curiosity and creativity.
    3. There are no soundproof spaces for reading.
    4. Objects are often randomly placed, and spaces are inefficiently used.
    5. Interaction is mainly analogous, with very limited number of digital interactions.
    6. Younger children have more chance to interact with different objects other than books as there are toys suited for that age group.
    7. Objects have often a single function and are not always tailored for children and are hard to repurpose.
    8. A library is a multifunctional place that goes beyond reading.
    9. There is a diversity in the demographics of children visiting each library.
    10. The demographic research by the library neglects the children.
    11. Children's wishes and complaints revolve around 4 main points: décor, furniture, space, book system.
    12. Some children use smart devices and others use social media.

    INSIGHTS

    PERSONA

    From our research, we noticed that children think that the library is a dull place as it does not accommodate their needs. As children grow older, they become more distant towards the library and only think of it as a functional place where they can borrow books. Therefore, we want to design an experience and a product that makes it possible for children aged 8 to 12 to trigger their imagination and give them a feeling of home, relatedness, excitement, and joy as they immerse themselves in a new world of books where they can expand their curiosity and creativity.

    FLOOR PLAN

    VIDEO PROTOTYPE

    • The library is decorated with the camping theme, which creates a different environment to stimulate interest in reading and joining the activities of the library.
    • The touch points (small tents, big tents, camping vans) provide a space for every individual’s needs while the children come to the library:
    -Private reading.
    -Group reading.
    -Group activities.
    • The concept provides a space for the children to socialize at the campfire.
    • The playground area has hammocks that bring in a place in which they can relax.
    • The technology used in the touchpoints is known to them and easy to use and understand.

    HOW BOOK CAMPING SOLVES THE PROBLEM

    WHY BOOK CAMPING IS THE SOLUTION

    • The design follows a modular approach making it flexible and applicable to libraries of different sizes.
    • The badge system is expandable depending on the activities the library incorporates.
    • The concept brings gamification to real life by using a reward system and ranking system similar to that of scouting using badges and other scouting items.
    • The scouting system encourages children to revisit the library as badges are earned over time by collecting stamps.
    • The book truck is a way to bring the library to the children and can be used for marketing and promotion purposes.
    • The concept supports multi-functionality as several of the touchpoints can serve different functions depending on the needs of the library.
    • Children with different interests have different places that meet their needs.
    • The concept focuses on the children who have different attitudes towards the library.
    • The concept uses a relatable theme that is suitable for children from different demographic backgrounds.
